Alexandria

Aswan

Aswan is one of the Nubian cities in Egypt and was considered its southern gate in the past. It is one of the oldest cities in Egypt, with many antiquities and ancient cultural landmarks, making it a distinctive tourist city.

Cairo

Cairo, founded in 969 AD, is the administrative capital of Egypt. Known as the "City of a Thousand Minarets," it is rich in history and culture, making it a must-visit destination.

King Tutankhamun

He was born in the twelfth year of the reign of King Akhenaten and has the royal cemetery of Akhenaten in Tell al-Amarna in Minya and cemetery 55 in the Valley of the Kings from his second wife, Queen Kia. He bore the name "Tutankhatun," which means in the ancient Egyptian language "the living image of the furnace."

The Citadel of Qaitbay

The Citadel of Qaitbay is one of the most important defensive fortresses on the Mediterranean coast. It was built by the Mamluk Sultan Al-Ashraf Abu Al-Nasr Qaitbay in the period between (882-884 AH / 1477-1479 AD) on the ruins of the ancient Lighthouse of Alexandria at the eastern end of Pharos Island (present-day Anfoushi) to fortify the city of Alexandria and protect it from naval invasions.

The Grand Egyptian Museum

The Grand Egyptian Museum is one of the largest museums in the world, and it is the largest museum in Egypt. It is located in the city of Giza, near the pyramids of Giza, and it is considered one of the most important tourist attractions in Egypt.
