3 Places Why You should Visit Now

Alexandera
By order of Alexander the Great, one of the most famous military leaders and conquerors throughout history, he decided to connect the passage on the coast to the edges of an island called (Banu Phanar). The order to establish this city came for several reasons; the most important of which is that Alexandria would become a center of trade, a center of Hellenistic civilization, and a shield to assist the Macedonian army in its conquests in the Mediterranean. It was classified as one of the Seven Wonders of the World.
Aswan
Aswan is one of the Nubian cities in Egypt, and was considered its southern gate in the past. Aswan is located on the eastern bank of the Nile River at the first cataract, and its population is approximately 1,480,284 people, according to statistics for 2016 AD. Aswan is considered one of the oldest cities in Egypt, and it has many antiquities and ancient cultural landmarks, which made it a distinctive tourist city.
Cairo
It is one of the cities of the Arab Republic of Egypt, which was founded in the year 969 AD, and is considered its administrative capital. It ranks first among Arab cities in terms of area, as its land area is 453 km², and it is 23 meters above sea level. The city has been called by many names, including: the city of a thousand minarets, the city of Egypt the Protected, and the city of Al-Muizz’s Cairo.

King Tutankhamun:

He was born in the twelfth year of the reign of King Akhenaten and has the royal cemetery of Akhenaten in Tell al-Amarna in Minya and cemetery 55 in the Valley of the Kings from his second wife, Queen Kia, who died during the birth of Prince Tut and bore the name "Tutankhatun" which means in the ancient Egyptian language "the living image of the furnace".
The Citadel of Qaitbay is one of the most important defensive fortresses on the Mediterranean coast. It was built by the Mamluk Sultan Al-Ashraf Abu Al-Nasr Qaitbay in the period between (882-884 AH / 1477-1479 AD) on the ruins of the ancient Lighthouse of Alexandria at the eastern end of Pharos Island (present-day Anfoushi) to fortify the city of Alexandria and protect it from naval invasions.
